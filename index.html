The digital plague of misinformation

The purpose of this website

This website aims to give a better understanding of the nature of misinformation by explaining its causes and effects. It also aims to encourage others to be wary of it by enumerating the benefits to the minimization of misinformation.

What is misinformation?

Misinformation, as defined by Merriam-Webster, is "incorrect or misleading information"

What causes it to spread?

To answer this question, we must talk of the motivation behind spreading misinformation and the means. People may be motivated to spread misinformation to paint a certain group in a good or bad light, perhaps in order to eke out some edge against said group. Alternatively, people may simply believe that the misinformation they're spreading is true, and genuinely have good, if misplaced, intentions. These motivations are further compounded by social media, giving anyone with a social media account the means by which to implant an idea into people of all communities.
